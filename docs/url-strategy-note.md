# tomcarr.com.au vs wombatloans.com.au — strategic note

You asked the right question. Here's my honest take.

## The short version

**Use both. Run them as a coordinated pair.** Personal-name URL as the front door for paid marketing and direct relationships; brand URL as the operational home for the business, podcast, articles, and referrals.

I'd go further than your "maybe just align the styles" instinct. If you're already considering this, lean in. The reasoning matters.

## Why this isn't a binary choice

Most of the people debating "personal brand vs business brand" are arguing about a constraint that doesn't exist. They're behaving like 1995 — when one URL meant one website meant one business. Today you can run a personal site and a business site that share infrastructure, share design, share content, and just *frame* the relationship differently for different audiences.

The relevant question isn't "which URL?" — it's "which job does each URL do?"

## What each URL is actually best at

**tomcarr.com.au**
- The first thing someone Googles when a friend says "you should talk to Tom"
- Cleaner landing page for paid social — Meta/LinkedIn ads built around your face and story convert better when they land somewhere that matches that promise
- Owns your name in search (you should own your own name on Google. Right now `tomcarr.com.au` going elsewhere or being parked is a vulnerability)
- The natural URL for your LinkedIn-driven inbound — your bio links there, your DMs go there
- Future-proof if you ever want to hire/sell — Tom Carr the person can keep his URL while the company evolves
- Honest signal: "you're getting Tom, not a brand"

**wombatloans.com.au** (or wombathomeloans.com.au — see below)
- The compliance-and-business home — ACL holder, ABN, terms, privacy
- The podcast home (`/podcast`) — Wombat Wrap is already a brand
- Article library / SEO content — articles ranked under wombatloans.com.au keep their authority
- Referral handoffs — easier for a buyer's agent or accountant to say "I send people to Wombat" than "I send people to Tom Carr's website"
- Future-proof in a different direction — if you ever bring on a second broker or a junior, the brand keeps working

## How they should relate

The simplest, cleanest version:

- **tomcarr.com.au** is the personal site. Hero is you. Story is you. The pitch is "work with me." The footer says "operated by Wombat Home Loans, ACL 561324."
- **wombatloans.com.au** stays close to its current structure but is reframed slightly as the *brand* hub: about Tom, the podcast, the article archive, the property search tool, the broader content engine.
- Both feed into the same Calendly. Both share the same design system, fonts, colours, photos. A visitor who jumps from one to the other should feel "yep, this is the same world."
- A small badge on each: "Part of Wombat Home Loans" / "Tom is the broker behind Wombat Home Loans."

This is exactly how Bench Accounting / Ian Crosby operated, how `naval.com` and AngelList co-existed, how `paulgraham.com` lives alongside YCombinator. It's how strong personal-brand-businesses work.

## The "no other broker does this" objection

You said it's "not one any typical broker takes." Correct. That's the *point*. Two reasons it works in your favour:

1. **Differentiation IS the strategy.** Your whole positioning is "I'm not a typical broker." The URL choice should reflect that, not contradict it.
2. **The reason most brokers don't do this is they don't have a story worth putting their name on.** They sell on rates, not relationships. Your competitive advantage is precisely the thing that makes a personal-name URL viable. Hiding behind "Wombat" is leaving your real edge unused.

The brokers I'd worry about copying are at the top end — buyer's agents and finance strategists who charge premium fees. *They* almost universally lead with their name (Cate Bakos, Ben Plohl, Chris Bates). It's not amateur — it's a confidence signal.

## The risks, and how to mitigate them

**Risk 1: Confusion / brand dilution.** Two URLs can confuse a casual visitor.
- *Mitigation:* explicit cross-linking, identical visual design, and a one-line "About" relationship on each ("Tom is the founder of Wombat Home Loans" / "Wombat is Tom's brokerage"). Confusion comes from inconsistency, not from having two URLs.

**Risk 2: SEO split.** Two domains can dilute search authority.
- *Mitigation:* don't duplicate content. tomcarr.com.au is short, focused, conversion-oriented. wombatloans.com.au keeps the article library and content depth. They serve different intents. Google handles this fine — it's a recognised pattern.

**Risk 3: Email / contact fragmentation.**
- *Mitigation:* one email forwards to the other. tom@tomcarr.com.au → tom@wombatloans.com.au, or vice versa. The user never sees the seam.

**Risk 4: It feels like overkill for a one-person shop.**
- This one's real. Don't do this if you're not prepared to maintain both. But — they don't both need to be giant sites. tomcarr.com.au can literally be a single landing page that mirrors the redesign I built. That's a weekend of work, not a project.

## On wombathomeloans.com.au vs wombatloans.com.au

You're already shutting down `wombathomeloans.com.au` in favour of `wombatloans.com.au`. Good — shorter, cleaner, easier to say. Don't let it lapse though. Set up a permanent 301 redirect to wombatloans.com.au (or to tomcarr.com.au, depending on which becomes primary). You'll keep the old SEO authority and stop anyone else snapping it up.

## What I'd actually do, in order

1. **Buy / secure tomcarr.com.au if you haven't already.** Then sit on it while you decide. Worst case, it's $20/year insurance.
2. **Decide your primary acquisition channel for the next 6 months.** If it's paid marketing → tomcarr.com.au is your landing page. If it's continued LinkedIn + podcast organic → wombatloans.com.au stays primary and tomcarr.com.au is a redirect or a one-pager.
3. **Build tomcarr.com.au as a focused single-page site** using the redesign we just made. 90% of the work is already done. It can literally be the homepage of the redesign, minus the article archive (which stays on wombatloans.com.au).
4. **Update wombatloans.com.au with the new design language** so the two feel like siblings. The current wombatloans.com.au is well-built but visually a generation behind the version we just designed.
5. **Cross-link everywhere.** Footer, About sections, internal references. Treat them as one experience with two front doors.
6. **Single Calendly. Single email. Single CRM.** The plumbing stays unified — only the marquee is split.

## My honest bias

If you're nervous about the boldness of leading with `tomcarr.com.au`, that nervousness is worth examining. Most of the "isn't this a bit much?" feeling around personal-brand decisions is calibrated to the broker-industry average — which is exactly the average you don't want to be. The brokers your ideal clients (expats, professionals, complex cases) actually choose between aren't your local franchise. They're a small set of high-trust operators, and those operators *all* use their names.

You've already done the hard part — you have a story that earns a personal-name URL. The remaining decision is just whether to use it.
